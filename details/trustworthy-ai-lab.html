Simulation-Augmented LLMs for Analog Circuit Design

Overview

This project explores the use of large language models for automated analog circuit and integrated circuit design. Analog circuit design traditionally requires substantial expertise from circuit designers, who need to translate design specifications into circuit topologies, select device connections, tune transistor sizing parameters, and repeatedly verify performance through SPICE simulation. This process is highly specialized, time-consuming, and difficult to fully automate.

Our goal is to develop a simulation-augmented AI for EDA pipeline where large language models can generate SPICE netlists from natural language circuit specifications and improve their designs based on simulation feedback. Instead of treating circuit generation as a pure text-generation task, this project connects LLM outputs with real circuit simulation, performance evaluation, and feedback-driven model training. The long-term goal is to train domain-specialized LLMs that can not only produce syntactically valid circuit descriptions, but also generate circuits that are functionally meaningful and performance-aware.
