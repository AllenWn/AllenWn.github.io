Internship · Deep Learning Algorithm Intern · Yuanli AI Co., Ltd. · Jun 2025 - Aug 2025

Post-training Educational LLMs for Math Reasoning and Visualization

Exploring post-training methods for educational LLMs to support math problem solving with visual explanations, focusing on structured drawing instructions and programmatic rendering for function plots, geometry figures, number lines, and word-problem illustrations.

This internship focused on post-training large language models for educational AI applications, especially in online learning and math problem-solving scenarios. Instead of relying on the traditional “photo-based question recognition + database answer retrieval” workflow, the goal was to build an LLM-based solving system that could understand mathematical problems, generate step-by-step reasoning, provide explanations, and support teaching-oriented interactions. The team developed a post-training pipeline based on open-source foundation models, including continued pretraining on educational corpora and supervised fine-tuning for downstream educational tasks such as question answering, problem solving, and writing assistance.

A major direction of this work was to explore how math-solving LLMs could generate visual explanations in addition to textual reasoning. In math education, diagrams such as function plots, geometry figures, number lines, line-segment diagrams, and word-problem illustrations can significantly improve the clarity of a solution. However, these images require precise mathematical structure, including coordinates, geometric relationships, labels, curve shapes, and proportional constraints. Because of this, open-ended image generation methods such as diffusion models are less suitable for this task, as their outputs are difficult to verify, reproduce, or align with structured mathematical reasoning.

To address this problem, the work adopted a “structured LLM output + programmatic rendering” approach. Instead of asking the model to directly generate final images, the model was trained to produce standardized drawing instructions, structured fields, or constrained rendering code during the reasoning process. These outputs were then parsed by a backend rendering system and converted into visual diagrams using Python-based plotting tools. This made the generated figures more controllable, reproducible, and easier to validate than free-form image generation.

The work involved defining the input-output format for math visualization tasks, selecting problem types that benefit from visual explanations, constructing SFT samples from math problems and standard solutions, designing structured drawing fields, and implementing the corresponding rendering pipeline. The resulting workflow connected LLM-based mathematical reasoning with rule-based visual generation, enabling AI-generated solutions to include both explanatory text and accurate supporting diagrams for educational use.
